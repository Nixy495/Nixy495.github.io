STS 2026 : The 4th Semi-supervised Teeth Segmentation Challenge on Metal Artifact Reduction and Beyond

MICCAI 2026, Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates

October 4-8, 2026

Evaluation Protocol

STS 2026 follows the three-task structure defined in the official challenge design document: Metal Artifact Reduction as the foundation task, Multi-structure Segmentation as the core analysis task, and Intraoral Scan-CBCT Registration as the fusion application task.

Evaluation Tracks

Track Submission Assessment
Track 1: Metal Artifact Reduction Artifact-reduced CBCT volumes for cases with crowns, bridges or implants. Restoration quality and downstream usability for dental analysis.
Track 2: Multi-structure Segmentation Segmentation masks for FDI-numbered teeth, dental implants and maxillary sinuses. DSC and HD95 are reported as independent ranking dimensions.
Track 3: Intraoral Scan-CBCT Registration Rigid transformation matrix aligning the IOS crown surface to the CBCT volume. MTE and MRE are used to measure geometric alignment accuracy.

Metrics

  • Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC): segmentation overlap.
  • 95% Hausdorff Distance (HD95): segmentation boundary accuracy.
  • Mean Translation Error (MTE): registration translation accuracy.
  • Mean Rotation Error (MRE): registration rotation accuracy.

DSC and HD95 are intentionally kept separate for Track 2. HD95 is the primary ranking criterion because metal artifact blooming mainly corrupts anatomical boundaries, and boundary fidelity is clinically important for artifact-affected CBCT analysis. DSC is used as the tiebreaker.

Ranking Rules

  • Track 1 evaluates metal artifact reduction as the foundation task for downstream CBCT analysis.
  • Track 2 reports Rank_DSC and Rank_HD95 separately, averaged across all segmentation targets and test cases.
  • The Track 2 winner is determined by Rank_HD95, with Rank_DSC used as the tiebreaker.
  • Track 3 ranks teams by registration accuracy using MTE and MRE.
  • Missing or failed test-case results receive the worst possible score, such as DSC=0 or HD95=infinity.

Dataset Split

Split Cases Provided Data
Training (Labeled) 40 CBCT, IOS, segmentation masks and registration matrices.
Training (Unlabeled) 219 Raw CBCT and IOS data for semi-supervised learning.
Validation 20 Raw CBCT and IOS data; ground truth withheld for server evaluation.
Test 100 Raw CBCT and IOS data; hidden ground truth for final ranking.
Total 379 All cases include metallic restorations and metal artifacts.

The labeled training set and test set share the same metal artifact severity stratification: 30% mild, 40% moderate and 30% severe. This mirrored distribution keeps the task definition stable and evaluates robustness to metal artifacts rather than unexpected domain shift.

Statistical Analysis

The organizers will estimate 95% confidence intervals using bootstrap analysis, compare top teams with paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, and report variability with standard deviation, interquartile range and box-and-whisker plots. Additional analyses will include artifact severity stratification, semi-supervised learning efficacy and inter-rater reliability by artifact subgroup.
