STS 2026 : The 4th Semi-supervised Teeth Segmentation Challenge on Metal Artifact Reduction and Beyond

MICCAI 2026, Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates

October 4-8, 2026

Call for Participation and Papers

STS 2026 invites teams to develop fully automatic artifact-resilient dental AI systems for metal artifact reduction, CBCT multi-structure segmentation and IOS-CBCT registration. The challenge is hosted on Codabench and uses Docker-based submissions for reproducible evaluation on the hidden test set.

Participation Policy

  • No user interaction is allowed during inference; submissions must run fully automatically.
  • Participants may use public external data and public pretrained weights, such as ImageNet or SAM, if the sources are cited and accessible at challenge launch.
  • Private non-public data is not prohibited, but it must be declared by the participating team.
  • Members of organizer institutes may participate, but they are not eligible for awards and will not be listed on the leaderboard.

Submission Method

Final submissions are Docker containers submitted through Codabench. Each container must package the inference code and runtime environment. A public validation phase will allow teams to check preliminary scores and Docker compatibility before the final test phase.

Awards and Announcement

  • Top 3 teams will be announced publicly at the workshop.
  • The full leaderboard will be made available online.
  • Top teams will receive certificates and will be invited to present at the STS 2026 satellite event.
  • Cash prizes will be confirmed based on sponsorship.

Publication Plan

Participants are expected to submit short methodology papers in LNCS format for the challenge proceedings. The organizers plan to coordinate a Springer LNCS proceedings volume and a comprehensive journal article summarizing the challenge design, dataset, methods, results and analysis. Top-performing and innovative teams may be invited as co-authors according to contribution.

Participating teams may publish their own methods independently after a 6-month embargo following the MICCAI conference, allowing the organizers to prepare the joint challenge paper first.

Reproducibility

To be eligible for final awards and inclusion in the challenge journal paper, winning teams must open-source both training and inference code and provide a Docker container for their algorithm. Evaluation code for DSC, HD95, MTE and MRE will be released through the official repository before the validation phase.

Ethics and Data Usage

Ethical approval was obtained from the Institutional Review Board of Sir Run Run Shaw Hospital, Zhejiang University School of Medicine (Approval Number: 2025-IRB-0136). The data were retrospectively collected, de-identified and approved for research use with informed consent waived.

Challenge data are released under CC BY-NC (Attribution-NonCommercial). Test labels remain confidential and are accessible only to the core organizing team for maintaining the evaluation server.

The latest public materials for STS 2026 will also be linked from the official GitHub repository.
